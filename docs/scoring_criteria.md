# Research: CV Scoring Tools & Developer Evaluation Criteria

## Top 3 Automated CV Scoring Tools (2024-2025)

Based on current market research, these are the top tools for automated resume screening and scoring, particularly relevant for technical recruitment:

1.  **Manatal**
    *   **Type:** AI Recruitment Software & ATS.
    *   **Key Features:** Uses AI to score candidates against job descriptions, providing a match percentage. It creates candidate profiles from various sources (LinkedIn, etc.) and offers recommendation engines.
    *   **Best For:** Streamlining the entire recruitment funnel with strong AI matching capabilities.

2.  **CVViZ**
    *   **Type:** AI Resume Screening Software.
    *   **Key Features:** Goes beyond keyword matching by using contextual understanding ("semantically similar" terms). It ranks candidates in real-time as they apply and can predict candidate longevity.
    *   **Best For:** Reducing bias and improving the quality of hire through "smart" ranking rather than simple keyword filtering.

3.  **HackerEarth (Assessment & Scoring)**
    *   **Type:** Technical Assessment & Interview Platform.
    *   **Key Features:** While widely known for coding tests, its "FaceCode" and screening layers provide deep technical scoring that generic ATS tools miss. It evaluates actual coding proficiency, logic, and problem-solving skills.
    *   **Best For:** **Hiring Developers Specifically.** It validates the "Hard Skills" claimed in a CV with verified scores.

## 5 Important Criteria for Evaluating a Developer CV

When configuring a scoring engine or manually reviewing a Developer CV, these are the 5 critical dimensions to weigh:

### 1. Tech Stack Matching (Hard Skills)
*   **Definition:** The degree of overlap between the candidate's skills and the role's core requirements.
*   **What to look for:**
    *   **Core Languages:** (e.g., Python, Java, TypeScript).
    *   **Frameworks:** (e.g., React, Spring Boot, Django).
    *   **Tools:** (e.g., Docker, Kubernetes, AWS/GCP).
*   **Scoring Weight:** **High**. This is usually the primary filter.

### 2. Practical Experience & Project Relevance (Depth)
*   **Definition:** Not just "Years of Experience", but the complexity and relevance of the projects handled.
*   **What to look for:**
    *   Evidence of working on similar systems (e.g., High-concurrency systems, Microservices, FinTech apps).
    *   Role played in the project (Lead, Contributor, Architect).
*   **Scoring Consideration:** 5 years of maintaining legacy code $\neq$ 5 years of building scalable cloud-native apps.

### 3. Problem Solving & Quantifiable Impact
*   **Definition:** Ability to deliver results, optimized performance, or solved business problems.
*   **What to look for:**
    *   **Metrics:** "Reduced API latency by 40%", "Scaled to 1M users", "Saved $10k/month in cloud costs".
    *   **Action-Oriented Verbs:** "Architected", "Optimized", "Refactored", "Solved".
*   **Why it matters:** Separates "doers" from "participants".

### 4. Technical Quality (Code & Portfolio)
*   **Definition:** Visible proof of coding standards and passion for technology.
*   **What to look for:**
    *   **GitHub/GitLab Profile:** Active contributions, clean code repositories, documentation quality.
    *   **Open Source:** Contributions to established libraries.
    *   **Link:** Personal technical blog or portfolio.

### 5. Domain Knowledge & Soft Skills context
*   **Definition:** Understanding of the business domain and ability to collaborate (especially for Senior+ roles).
*   **What to look for:**
    *   **Domain:** Experience in similar sectors (e.g., Banking, E-commerce, Healthcare).
    *   **Soft Skills:** Mentorship experience, cross-functional collaboration, Agile/Scrum leadership.
    *   **Education:** Relevant Degree (CS/Engineering) or specialized Certifications (AWS Certified Solutions Architect).
